Dear Leader,
We say with pride that leadership at WiseTechis different. We lead others, manage 
ourselves and look to our People Leaders for inspiration, support, guidance, and 
encouragement.
As a People Leader, it is important to lead by example and role model the behaviors 
that we expect from our team members. Managing ourselves when leading others is 
a core component of our culture and way of working. As such, being conscious of 
making the right choices, exhibiting the right behaviors, establishing trust, and 
communicating openly are essential to leadership at WiseTech. It is also important 
to ensure that failures are converted to learning and growth opportunities, and that 
team members' development and career aspirations are supported.

something to offer for everyone.

exercises to help you apply the concepts and techniques we cover to real-world 
scenarios. You will have the opportunity to work collaboratively with your fellow 
participants and learn from one another's experiences. This journal will come in 
handy for you to make important notes (and revisit them later) and set actions for 
self. 
Let's work towards becoming the best leaders we can be!

professional settings. Strong leadership can drive positive change,

increasingly diverse and complex workplace. Leaders who understand 
and embrace diversity and inclusion can foster a culture of respect, 
collaboration, and innovation, and better serve the needs of their teams 
and customers.
Overall, developing leadershipskills is a valuable investment for 
individuals and organizations alike, with the potential to drive growth, 
enhance performance, and create positive change.

Overview of common leadership 
styles:
There are many different leadership 
styles, each with its own strengths and 
weaknesses.
Some common styles include 
autocratic, democratic, 
transformational, servant, and laissez-
faire leadership.
Autocratic leaders tend to make 
decisions independently and exert 
control over their teams, while 
democratic leaders involve team 
members in decision-making and 
prioritize collaboration.
Transformational leaders focus on 
inspiring and motivating team members 
to achieve a shared vision, while servant 
leaders prioritize serving the needs of 
others.
Laissez-faire leaders give their teams a 
lot of autonomy and freedom, but may 
be less involved in decision-making and 
oversight.
What do you think is your current leadership style? How is it impacting team 
dynamics?

Building Trust

--- PAGE 12 ---
© 2020 WiseTech Global12
Understanding the importance 
of trust:
Trust is essential for effective 
leadership and teamwork, as it 
allows team members to work 
together effectively and take 
risks without fear of judgement 
or betrayal.
Building trust takes time and 
effort, but is essential for 
creating a positive and 
productive work environment.
Trust is built through consistent 
actions and behaviors that 
demonstrate honesty, 
transparency, and reliability.
Exercises to practice building trust in the team:
Sharing personal stories:
• Encourage team members to share personal stories or experiences that have shaped 
their values, beliefs, or work ethic. This helps build empathy and understanding among 
team members and creates a sense of vulnerability that can lead to increased trust.
Group problem-solving:
• Assign a complex problem or challenge to the team and have them work together to 
come up with a solution. This requires everyone to trust each other's expertise and 
judgement, and promotes collaboration and teamwork.
Peer recognition:
• Set aside time during team meetings to recognize and celebrate individual team 
member's contributions and accomplishments. This promotes a culture of appreciation 
and recognition, and can help build trust and support among team members.
Trust-building activities:
• There are many trust-building activities available online, such as trust falls or human 
knots. These exercises are designed to help team members build trust by relying on 
each other and working together to achieve a common goal.

© 2020 WiseTech Global13
Maintaining trust over time:
Building and maintaining trust is an 
ongoing process that requires 
consistent effort and attention.
Continue to communicate openly 
and transparently with team 
members, and hold yourself and 
others accountable.
Be aware of potential triggers or 
situations that could damage trust 
and take proactive steps to address 
them before they become a problem.
Regularly check in with team 
members and solicit feedback to 
ensure that everyone feels heard 
and valued.

Empathy and Emotional Intelligence

--- PAGE 16 ---
© 2020 WiseTech Global16
Understanding the 
importance of empathy:
Empathy is the ability to 
understand and share the 
feelings of others, and is a crucial 
skill for effective leadership and 
team collaboration.
Leaders who are able to 
demonstrate empathy create a 
positive work environment where 
team members feel understood 
and supported, which can lead to 
increased motivation, 
productivity, and job satisfaction.
Empathy helps build trust and 
rapport among team members, 
and promotes effective 
communication and conflict 
resolution.
Exercises to practice building Empathy in the team:
Role-playing exercises:
• Create scenarios that allow team members to practice putting themselves in others' 
shoes and responding with empathy. This can help team members develop their 
empathy skills and improve their ability to communicate effectively.
Active listening exercises:
• Practice active listening by having team members pair up and take turns sharing their 
thoughts and feelings while the other person listens and validates their emotions. This 
can help build trust and promote understanding among team members.
Gratitude exercises:
• Encourage team members to practice gratitude by reflecting on what they appreciate 
about each other and sharing their thoughts during team meetings. This can help build a 
sense of connection and empathy among team members.
Storytelling exercises:
• Encourage team members to share personal stories or experiences that have shaped 
their perspectives and values. This can help build empathy and understanding among 
team members and promote a sense of shared experience.

Understanding the importance of 
emotional intelligence:
Emotional intelligence is the ability 
to recognize, understand, and 
manage one's own emotions, as 
well as the emotions of others. It is 
a critical skill for effective 
leadership, as it helps leaders to 
build rapport and trust with their 
team members, and to manage 
conflicts and difficult 
conversations.
Leaders who possess high 
emotional intelligence are more 
effective at communicating with 
and motivating their team 
members, which can lead to 
increased job satisfaction and 
productivity.
Emotional intelligence also helps 
leaders to manage their own stress 
and emotions, which can lead to 
improved decision-making and 
problem-solving abilities.
Exercises to practice Emotional Intelligence in the team:
Emotional self-awareness exercise:
• Take some time each day to reflect on your own emotions and how they may be 
impacting your behavior and interactions with others. Write down your thoughts and 
feelings in a journal to help increase your self-awareness.
Active listening and empathy exercise:
• Practice active listening by giving your full attention to team members and taking the 
time to understand their perspectives and emotions. Try to put yourself in their shoes 
and consider their point of view.
Communication exercise:
• Practice clear and concise communication by writing down a message you need to 
convey to a team member. Then, try to condense the message into a single sentence or 
phrase, without losing any important information.
Stress management exercise:
• Practice stress management techniques, such as deep breathing or meditation, to help 
you stay calm and composed in stressful situations. Take some time each day to 
practice these techniques and reflect on how they make you feel.

Understanding 
psychological safety:
Psychological safety is a 
belief that a team is a safe 
place to take interpersonal 
risks and speak up without 
fear of negative 
consequences.
A psychologically safe team 
is one where members feel 
comfortable sharing their 
thoughts, ideas, and 
concerns, without fear of 
ridicule, punishment, or 
rejection.
Psychological safety is 
critical for effective team 
collaboration, innovation, and 
creativity, and is a key driver 
of team performance and 
productivity.
Exercises to practice promoting Psychological safety in the team:
Icebreaker exercise:
• Start each team meeting with an icebreaker activity, such as a question of the day or a team-
building exercise. This can help to create a sense of community and encourage team 
members to open up and share their thoughts and ideas.
Feedback exercise:
• Encourage team members to give each other feedback on a regular basis. Provide a 
structured feedback framework that promotes constructive criticism, such as the 
"compliment sandwich" method, where positive feedback is sandwiched between areas for 
improvement.
Team-building exercise:
• Plan a team-building activity that encourages team members to work together and support 
each other. For example, a team-building scavenger hunt or a group project where everyone 
has a role to play.
Empathy exercise:
• Encourage team members to practice empathy by putting themselves in each other's shoes 
and considering their perspectives and emotions. Have team members pair up and take turns 
sharing a personal story or experience, and encourage active listening and empathy-building 
skills.

safety in action:
Create an open-door policy, and 
encourage team members to

provide opportunities for 
everyone to share their thoughts

learn and grow.
Encourage team members to

Understanding Diversity, 
Inclusion, and Belonging:
Diversity refers to the 
differences in backgrounds, 
experiences, and 
perspectives that team 
members bring to the table.
Inclusion is the practice of 
actively valuing and 
respecting diversity by 
creating a work environment 
that welcomes and 
embraces different 
perspectives and 
backgrounds.
Belonging is the feeling that 
team members have when 
they feel included, valued, 
and respected for who they 
are.
Exercises to promote DIB in the team:
Diverse Perspective Sharing:
• Have team members take turns sharing their personal experiences and perspectives 
related to a work issue or project. Encourage active listening and empathy-building 
skills.
Diversity Training:
• Provide a training session on diversity and inclusion, with a focus on unconscious bias, 
cultural sensitivity, and creating an inclusive workplace.
Inclusive Meeting Facilitation:
• When leading a team meeting, encourage everyone to participate and share their 
perspectives. Avoid interrupting or dominating the conversation, and make sure that 
everyone feels heard.
Cultural Awareness Activities:
• Plan team-building activities that celebrate different cultures and perspectives, such as 
potluck lunches featuring different ethnic cuisines or team outings to cultural festivals.

Understanding Allyship:
Allyship is the practice of 
supporting and advocating 
for members of marginalized 
or underrepresented groups, 
using one's privilege and 
power to help promote 
equity and inclusion.
Allies recognize and 
challenge their own biases 
and privileges, and take 
action to create more 
equitable and inclusive 
environments.
Exercises to promote Allyship in the team:
Privilege Walk:
Lead a privilege walk exercise where team members stand in a line and take steps 
forward or backward based on privilege and power-related statements read aloud. The 
exercise can raise awareness about privilege and power dynamics.
Bias Awareness and Challenge:
Encourage team members to challenge their own biases and engage in self-reflection 
about their privilege and power, by offering a training or group activity that explores the 
concept of unconscious bias.
Community Outreach and Volunteering:
Encourage team members to participate in community outreach and volunteering 
activities, including supporting local organizations that promote equity and inclusion.

actions, and encourage others to

group members, and promote 
their work and achievements.
Take action to create more 
inclusive and equitable policies 
and practices, such as advocating 
for more diverse hiring practices 
or supporting employee resource 
groups.
Attend and support events and 
initiatives that promote equity 
and inclusion, both inside and 
outside of work.

--- PAGE 33 ---
© 2020 WiseTech Global33
Strategies for Leading Cross-
Cultural Teams:
Build cultural competence by 
learning about the cultural 
backgrounds, communication 
styles, and values of team 
members from different cultures.
Foster an inclusive team culture 
that values diversity, and creates 
opportunities for team members 
to share their perspectives and 
experiences.
Communicate effectively by 
adapting your communication 
style to meet the needs and 
expectations of team members 
from different cultures.
Encourage cross-cultural learning 
and exchange by creating 
opportunities for team members 
to learn from one another's 
experiences and perspectives.
Exercises to practice Leading cross-culture teams:
Cultural Icebreaker:
• At the start of team meetings, ask each team member to share one interesting fact 
about their culture or background. This exercise can help team members learn about 
each other's cultural backgrounds and start building rapport.
Culture Map:
• Ask each team member to create a cultural map that highlights their cultural 
background, values, and communication style. This exercise can help team members 
better understand and appreciate each other's cultural differences and similarities.
Cross-Cultural Learning Exchange:
• Pair up team members from different cultural backgrounds and ask them to share their 
experiences and perspectives on a particular topic. This exercise can help team 
members learn from one another's experiences and build cross-cultural awareness.
Diversity and Inclusion Action Plan:
• Develop an action plan with your team that outlines specific strategies and actions to 
promote diversity, inclusion, and belonging within the team. This exercise can help team 
members take concrete steps towards creating a more inclusive and equitable work 
environment.

Understand Cultural 
Differences: 
In order to communicate 
effectively across cultures, it 
is important to understand 
and respect the cultural 
differences. Different 
cultures have different 
communication styles, 
customs, and traditions. 
Take time to learn about the 
culture of the person or 
team you are 
communicating with. This 
can include learning about 
their communication style, 
customs, beliefs, values, and 
taboos.
Exercises to practice effective communication across cultures:
Pair and Share: 
Pair team members from different cultural backgrounds and ask them to share their 
experiences of working in a cross-cultural team. Encourage them to share their 
communication styles, customs, and values. This can help team members to better 
understand and appreciate each other's perspectives.
Communication Exercises: 
Conduct communication exercises that challenge team members to communicate 
effectively across cultures. For example, you can ask team members to communicate a 
message using different communication styles or ask them to use non-verbal 
communication to convey a message.
Role-Playing: 
Conduct role-playing exercises that simulate cross-cultural communication scenarios. Ask 
team members to play different roles and communicate effectively in a cross-cultural 
setting. This can help team members to better understand and appreciate the challenges of 
cross-cultural communication.

Be Mindful of Language Barriers:
Language barriers can often 
hinder effective communication 
across cultures. Be mindful of 
your language and use simple, 
clear language that is easy to 
understand. Avoid using slang, 
idioms, and technical jargon that 
may not be familiar to the other 
person. If possible, try to learn 
some key phrases in the other 
person's language to show that 
you are making an effort to 
communicate with them.

Understanding the 
difference between 
individual contributor and 
leadership roles: 
As an individual contributor, 
your focus was on achieving 
your own goals and 
delivering results on your 
assigned tasks. However, as 
a leader, you need to focus 
on supporting and guiding 
your team members to 
achieve their goals, while 
ensuring that the team 
delivers results that align 
with the organization's goals.
Exercises to practice the transition:
Role play exercises: 
Practice different scenarios where you have to communicate with team members, colleagues, 
and senior leaders as a manager. This can include practicing how to provide feedback, deliver 
presentations, or handle difficult conversations.
Delegation exercises: 
Practice delegating tasks to team members and providing feedback on their performance. 
This will help you develop the skills to delegate tasks effectively, manage your team's 
workload, and provide feedback that motivates and improves their performance.
Team building exercises: 
Practice building a high-performing team by engaging in team building exercises that 
promote collaboration, communication, and teamwork. This can include activities like 
brainstorming sessions, team-building games, or group problem-solving exercises.
Time management exercises: 
Practice managing your time effectively by setting goals, prioritizing tasks, and scheduling 
your work. This can include exercises like time tracking, task prioritization exercises, or 
calendar management exercises.

Giving and Receiving Feedback

--- PAGE 44 ---
© 2020 WiseTech Global44
Importance of feedback:
Feedback is a critical tool 
for growth and 
development, both for 
individuals and teams. 
Effective feedback can help 
team members understand 
their strengths and areas for 
improvement, and can lead 
to improved performance 
and results. It is important 
for leaders to provide 
regular feedback and to 
create a culture where 
feedback is valued and 
expected.
Exercises to practice giving and receiving feedback:
Feedback survey: 
Have team members complete an anonymous feedback survey about their performance or 
the team's performance, and use the results to guide a group discussion about areas for 
improvement and action plans for growth.
Feedback journal: 
Have team members keep a journal where they reflect on feedback they receive and how 
they plan to use it for growth and development.
Feedback challenge: 
Set a challenge for team members to give and receive feedback at least once a week for a 
set period of time (e.g. a month). Encourage them to provide specific and constructive 
feedback, and to approach feedback with a growth mindset.

--- PAGE 45 ---
© 2020 WiseTech Global45
Giving feedback: 
When giving feedback, it is important 
to be specific, objective, and timely. 
Start with positive feedback to 
establish a positive tone, and then 
move on to areas where 
improvement is needed. Use specific 
examples to illustrate your points, 
and avoid making personal attacks or 
criticisms. Finally, work with the team 
member to create an action plan for 
improvement.
Receiving feedback:
Receiving feedback can be 
challenging, as it can be difficult to 
hear criticism or areas for 
improvement. However, it is 
important to approach feedback 
with an open mind and a growth 
mindset. Listen actively to the 
feedback, ask clarifying questions, 
and seek to understand the 
perspective of the person giving 
feedback. Finally, use the feedback 
as an opportunity for growth and 
improvement.

overcome
