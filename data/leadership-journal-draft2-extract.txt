Welcome to Leadership Foundations
Dear Leader,
We say with pride that leadership at WiseTech is different. We lead others, manage ourselves and look to our People Leaders for inspiration, support, guidance, and encouragement.
As a People Leader, it is important to lead by example and role model the behaviors we expect from our team members. Managing ourselves when leading others is a core component of our culture and way of working. As such, being conscious of making the right choices, role modelling the right behaviors, establishing trust, and communicating openly are essential to leadership at WiseTech. It is also important to ensure that failures are converted to learning and growth opportunities, and that team members' development and career aspirations are supported.
This program is designed to help you develop the foundational skills and knowledge necessary to be a successful leader. Whether you are just starting your leadership journey or are looking to enhance existing skills, this program has something to offer all.
Throughout the program, we will engage in interactive discussions, activities, and exercises to apply concepts and techniques, giving context and real-world scenarios. You will have the opportunity to work collaboratively with your fellow participants, sharing knowledge and learning from one another's experiences. 
This journal is intended to serve as a log for your learning, reflections, important notes (and revisit them later) and set actions for yourself. 
Let's learn, challenge ourselves and continue our journey to being the best leaders we can! 
Good luck!
How to use the Leadership Journal?
Start by reading through the journal to familiarize yourself with all the topics covered and how they relate to leadership development.
The journal covers a wide range of topics.  It is very beneficial to follow the topics alongside the Leadership Foundations; reading through the sections on each leadership topic and reflecting on how the insights and strategies presented can add value to your leadership role. 
Use the self-assessment quiz to identify your personal leadership style. This will help you understand your strengths and weaknesses as a leader and guide the development of your skills.
Complete the exercises are provided for each topic. The exercises designed to help you reflect, develop and practice your leadership skills in a practical way.
Make additional notes as you explore the topics and identify key actions for yourself. This also an opportunity to identify goals for your PDP and to think about how you can achieve them over a period of time. 
This journal is your document, make it personal to yourself, your learning style and your leadership journey.
Importance of developing Leadership Skills
Leadership skills are crucial for success in both personal and professional settings. Strong leadership can drive positive change, inspire and motivate others, and help individuals and organizations reach their goals. 
Leadership skills can be developed through a combination of training, experience, and self-reflection. Effective leadership requires a range of competencies, including good communication, decision-making, problem-solving, strategic thinking, empathy, and self-awareness.
Developing leadership skills can make individuals more effective in their roles and better equipped to lead and influence others, resulting in improved performance, increased job satisfaction, and greater opportunities for career advancement.
Furthermore, strong leadership skills are essential in today's increasingly diverse and complex workplace. Leaders who understand and embrace diversity and inclusion can foster a culture of respect, collaboration, creativity and innovation, and better serve the needs of their teams and customers.
Overall, developing leadership skills is a valuable investment for individuals and organizations alike, with the potential to drive growth, navigate change, enhance performance and create positive change.

Building Trust: Understanding the importance of trust
Trust is essential for effective leadership and teamwork, as it allows team members to work together effectively and take risks without fear of judgement or betrayal.
Building trust takes time and effort, but is essential for creating a positive and productive relationships and work environment.
Trust is built through consistent actions and behaviors that demonstrate honesty, transparency, and reliability.

Understanding the importance of emotional intelligence:
Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognize, understand, and manage one's own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. It is a critical skill for effective leadership, as it helps leaders to build rapport and trust with their team members, and to manage conflict and difficult conversations.
Leaders who possess high emotional intelligence are more effective at communicating  and motivating their team members, which can lead to increased job satisfaction and productivity.
Emotional intelligence also helps improve leaders self-management, making them better at managing their own stress and emotions, which can result in improved decision-making and problem-solving abilities.

Understanding the importance of empathy:
Empathy is the ability to understand the thoughts and feelings of another from their point of view, and is a crucial skill for effective leadership and team collaboration.
Leaders who are able to demonstrate empathy create a positive work environment where team members feel understood and supported, which can lead to increased motivation, productivity, and job satisfaction.
Empathy helps build trust and rapport among team members, and promotes effective communication and conflict resolution.

Understanding psychological safety:
Psychological safety is about candour and willingness to engage in productive conflict so as to learn from different points of view.
A psychologically safe team is one where members are comfortable sharing their thoughts, ideas, and concerns, without fear of ridicule, punishment, or rejection.
Psychological safety is essential for effective team collaboration, innovation, and creativity, and is a key driver of team performance and productivity.
Exercises to promote inclusivity in the team:Icebreaker exercise:Start each team meeting with an icebreaker activity, such as a light-hearted question for all to answer or a team-building exercise. Agile Culture:
Agile culture is a set of values, principles, and practices that aim to create an environment where individuals and teams can collaborate, innovate, and adapt quickly to changing circumstances. It is a culture that values continuous learning, experimentation, and improvement, as well as transparency, trust, and collaboration.
For leaders, Agile culture is important because it enables them to create an environment that fosters innovation, creativity, and continuous improvement. Leaders who embrace Agile culture are able to build and lead high-performing teams that are more responsive to change and better equipped to deliver value to customers.
Exercises to practice creating Agile culture:Daily Stand-up Meetings: A daily stand-up meeting is a quick team check-in that promotes open communication, transparency, and accountability. Each team member briefly shares their progress, roadblocks, and plans for the day. This exercise encourages collaboration, helps identify issues early, and ensures that everyone is aligned and moving in the same direction.Retrospectives: A retrospective is a team meeting held at the end of a sprint or project, where team members reflect on what went well, what could have been improved, and what actions they can take to improve in the future. This exercise promotes continuous improvement, helps identify areas for growth, and fosters a culture of learning and innovation.User Story Mapping: User story mapping is a visual exercise that helps teams define the scope of a project, identify user needs, and prioritize features. Giving feedback: 
When giving feedback, it is important to be specific, objective, and timely. Start with positive feedback to establish a positive tone, and then move on to areas where improvement is needed. Use specific examples to illustrate your points, and avoid making personal attacks or criticisms. Finally, work with the team member to create an action plan for improvement.
Receiving feedback:
Receiving feedback regularly can be a valuable source of insight into your leadership and the impact you have. While it may be challenging to hear about your areas for improvement or when things did not go well, turning these into a learning opportunity can be beneficial for building trust, demonstrating openness and a growth mindset. 
Positive feedback will help you understand the behaviours and actions that your team members appreciate and respect you for. 
Listen actively to the feedback, ask clarifying questions, and seek to understand the perspective of the person giving feedback. Finally, use the feedback as an opportunity for growth and improvement.

Activity based on GROW Model:
GROW is an acronym that stands for Goals, Realities, Options, and Will, and it has several purposes. It helps you and your employees clarify their initial goals, assess what’s currently happening, and identify potential roadblocks. It also helps your people brainstorm both opportunities and resources for growth.
Goals:  
• What is it you would like to focus on?  
• What would you like to achieve?  
• What would you like to happen that is not happening now?  
• How would you know you were being successful if you achieved your goal?  
• How could you break this goal down into manageable chunks?  
• What are all the things that would need to be done to achieve the goal?  
Realities - Assess what’s currently going on:  
• What is happening at the moment to derail your progress toward the goal?  
• When and how often does this happen? Be precise if possible.  
• What effect does this have?  
• What other factors are relevant?  
• Who else is relevant?  
• What is that person’s perception of the situation?  
• What have you tried so far?  
• What else is conflicting with achieving the goal?  
Options - Kick-start some brainstorming:  
• What possibilities for action do you see? Don’t worry about how realistic they are at this stage.  
• Who might be able to help?  
• Which options do you like the most?  
• What are the benefits and pitfalls of these options?  
• Which options are of interest to you?  
• Rate from 1 to 10 your assessment of the practicality of each of these options.  
• Would you like to choose an option to act on?  
Will - Unlock actions, accountabilities, and roadblocks:  
• What are your next steps?  
• Precisely when will you take them?  
• What might get in the way?
