# Leadership Principles
category: principles

Managers are expected to create clarity for their teams. Clarity means people understand priorities, decision boundaries, ownership, and what success looks like.

Leaders should combine support with accountability. Coaching does not replace standards. Strong leaders make expectations explicit, give timely feedback, and follow through on commitments.

Delegation is a development tool, not just a workload tool. Leaders should delegate outcomes and guardrails, then avoid taking back the work too early.
