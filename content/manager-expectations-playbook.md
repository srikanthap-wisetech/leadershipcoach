# Manager Expectations Playbook
category: playbook

When performance concerns arise, managers should describe the observed behavior, explain the impact on the team or work, and align on the next expected behavior.

Feedback should be specific, timely, and respectful. Managers should avoid storing up feedback until formal review cycles when a quicker conversation would help.

During team growth, leaders should invest in regular check-ins, clear prioritization, and coaching conversations that help team members build judgment.
