LeadWise Walkthrough Script

Hello everyone, and welcome to LeadWise.

LeadWise is a leadership learning portal built around WiseTech Leadership Foundations. It combines structured leadership learning, reflection, and community discussion in one place, so leaders can keep returning to it over time.

When I land on the homepage, the first thing I see is a branded LeadWise landing page with the WiseTech logo, the leadership mantra, and clear entry points into the journal, the community, My Activity, and Actions. From there, I can move into the experience I need in the moment.

I can also open My Activity, which gives me a personal view of my notes, conversations, ratings, followed threads, and recent actions across the portal.

There is also a dedicated Actions page, which brings together Give Feedback, Ask a Question, and Suggest New Topics in one place so leaders can contribute easily.

In the journal, all leadership topics are visible in a clean navigation panel, which makes it easy to move between topics like Leadership Styles, Delegation, Building Trust, Empathy and Emotional Intelligence, Psychological Safety, or Giving and Receiving Feedback.

The journal also includes a search bar, so leaders can quickly find a topic they want instead of manually scanning the full list.

When I click into a topic, the content opens in the main section. Each topic begins with a clear overview, followed by structured subtopics, practical explanations, real workplace examples, and a case study. The idea is to make the content feel usable in day-to-day leadership, not just theoretical.

For each topic, leaders can also engage more actively with the material. There is a quiz section for quick knowledge checks, exercises for application, and space to save personal notes and reflections. These notes can help leaders connect the content directly to situations in their own teams.

Below each topic, there are also suggested courses and recommended reading. These give leaders a way to continue learning beyond the journal itself, using curated resources from platforms like LinkedIn Learning and Coursera, as well as selected books.

At the bottom of each topic, leaders can rate the topic using a simple five-star rating. If a low rating is given, the tool automatically asks for more detailed feedback so the content can continue improving. The tool also shows the average rating from all users, so over time it becomes easier to see which topics are landing most effectively.

LeadWise also includes a Community space. This is a thread-based discussion area where leaders can post questions, start conversations, respond to one another, and support helpful contributions. It gives leaders the opportunity to learn not only from the journal content, but also from the lived experiences of their peers.

The community includes a conversation search as well, which makes it easier to find threads by topic, title, or discussion content.

Leaders can also follow a thread they care about. When a new reply is added to that conversation, the platform can queue an email-style alert for the followers of that thread, which makes it easier for discussions to stay active over time.

Another important feature is topic suggestions. If a leader feels an important topic is missing, they can suggest a new one directly from the Actions page. That suggestion then goes through a review workflow, where administrators and the people leadership team can review it, assign it, approve it, and eventually curate content for future release.

Overall, LeadWise is designed to be more than a content library. It is a leadership platform that combines structured learning, reflection, shared discussion, and continuous improvement.

Thank you for taking the time to walk through LeadWise.
